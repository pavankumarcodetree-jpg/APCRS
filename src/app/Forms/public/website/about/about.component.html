Civil Registration System (CRS) in Andhra Pradesh

The Civil Registration System (CRS) in Andhra Pradesh has its roots in the broader history of civil registration in India. This system ensures the mandatory and systematic registration of births, deaths, and stillbirths in the state, aligning with national laws and objectives.

Historical Background

The CRS traces its origins to the mid-19th century, when the Central Births, Deaths, and Marriages Registration Act of 1886 was introduced during British rule, making registration voluntary. Post-independence, the Government of India enacted the Registration of Births and Deaths Act (RBD Act), 1969, to standardize and mandate the registration process across the country.
